# Adjustment Methods Table Draft

Last updated: 2026-04-20

This draft is package-specific. It includes only methods currently implemented in `debiasR`.

Not currently implemented in the package and therefore excluded from the table:

- `Calibration (Zagheni framework)`
- `Difference-in-Differences`
- `Bayesian Data Fusion`

| Method | Rationale | Equation | Benchmark population | Active users | Benchmark OD flows | Digital OD flows | Covariates | Assumptions | Strengths | Weaknesses | Suitability for OD flows | Validation strategy |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| `adjust_inverse_penetration()` | Inflate observed flows using population-to-user ratios where coverage is thin. Good transparent baseline when bias is mainly under-coverage. | `w_i = P_i / U_i`; `F_adj_ij = F_mpd_ij * w_i`, `w_j`, or `sqrt(w_i w_j)` | Yes | Yes | No | Yes | No | Coverage bias is roughly proportional to penetration; origin/destination weights capture most bias. | Simple, transparent, cheap, no benchmark OD needed. | Can overinflate sparse areas; misses compositional and structural selection bias. | Good baseline for observed OD correction; weaker for nuanced structural bias. | `validate_flow_overall()` for fit metrics; `validate_flow_pairs()` to inspect where weights create large OD changes. |
| `adjust_selection_rate()` | Combine penetration with an income-like covariate to model differential selection, with optional benchmark calibration. Useful when simple inverse weighting is too crude. | `w_i(r_t) = 1 / (I_i p_i + (1 - I_i) r_t)`; `F_adj_ij = F_mpd_ij * w_ij` | Yes | Yes | Optional | Yes | Yes | Income-like covariate is informative; a global `r_t` is meaningful; benchmark OD is representative if used for calibration. | More flexible than inverse penetration; can incorporate covariates and benchmark calibration. | More tuning and interpretation burden; depends on covariate quality and calibration choice. | Good for OD flows where selection varies systematically across places. | Compare calibrated and uncalibrated outputs with `validate_flow_overall()`; use `validate_flow_pairs()` to inspect where calibration helps or harms. |
| `adjust_selection_rate2()` | Apply a closed-form penetration correction with optional `k` calibration. Useful as a parsimonious selection-style alternative. | `CF(p; k) = p * (exp(-k) - 1) / (exp(-k p) - 1)`; `F_adj_ij = F_mpd_ij * CF(...)` | Yes | Yes | Optional | Yes | No | A single curvature parameter `k` can summarize selection bias; penetration is measured well. | Compact, interpretable, lighter than method 2, can be calibrated. | Less flexible than method 2; may underfit heterogeneous bias. | Good lightweight OD correction when coverage is the dominant concern. | Use `validate_flow_overall()` for summary performance and `validate_flow_pairs()` for OD-level residual patterns. |
| `adjust_raking_ratio()` | Force adjusted OD flows to match trusted origin and/or destination margins. Best when consistency with benchmark totals is the main priority. | `F_adj_ij = F_mpd_ij * w_ij` subject to `sum_j F_adj_ij = O_i` and/or `sum_i F_adj_ij = D_j` | Optional (as user-built targets) | No | Yes or user-supplied targets | Yes | No | Benchmark marginals are correct; observed OD support is adequate; zero cells need not be created. | Ensures consistency with trusted marginals; clear and widely understood. | Cannot create missing OD support; may improve totals more than internal OD realism. | Very good for OD tables when marginal consistency matters most. | Check convergence and margin matching, then use `validate_flow_overall()` and `validate_flow_pairs()` on the adjusted table. |
| `adjust_coefficient()` | Learn a global mapping from MPD flows to benchmark OD flows, then apply it back to MPD. Best when benchmark OD is available and calibration is the main goal. | `E[F_bench_ij] = beta * F_mpd_ij` or `log(mu_ij) = alpha + log(F_mpd_ij)`; `F_adj_ij = beta_hat * F_mpd_ij` | No | No | Yes | Yes | No | Relationship between MPD and benchmark flows is stable enough to estimate; overlapping positive support exists. | Direct benchmark-driven calibration; multiple model families available. | Benchmark quality is critical; a global coefficient may miss local heterogeneity. | Strong when benchmark OD exists and a simple calibrated mapping is acceptable. | Use `validate_flow_overall()` across families and levels; use `validate_flow_pairs()` to inspect systematic residuals by OD pair. |
| `adjust_multilevel_bayes()` | Fit a hierarchical count model with coverage bias, covariates, and optional distance, then remove the estimated coverage-bias contribution from posterior fixed-effect predictors. Current scope is observed OD pairs only. | `log(mu_obs_ij) = alpha + beta_o X_i + beta_d X_j + gamma log(d_ij) + omega e_i + u`; Stage 1 returns `flow_adj = summary(exp(lin_fix_ij - omega e_i))` | Yes | Yes | No | Yes | Yes | Model family and hierarchy are well specified; coverage bias is captured by the fitted bias term; Stage 1 only adjusts observed OD rows. | Partial pooling, uncertainty-aware, richer structure, no benchmark OD needed for fitting. | Prototype, slower, dependency-heavy, and not yet a full missing-OD reconstruction method. | Promising for noisy or sparse observed OD correction; not yet ready for full OD completion. | Posterior predictive checks plus `validate_flow_overall()` on observed OD rows; use `validate_flow_pairs()` for row-level audit of adjusted outputs. |
